# Facilitation Guide

How to run the breakout discussion groups.

---

## Setup

- Groups of 5–8 people work best
- Seat people at tables or in circles — face-to-face matters
- If using the app, display questions on a shared screen: [conversation-prompter](../app/README.md)
- If not using the app, print question cards or write questions on a whiteboard

---

## Role of the facilitator

Every group benefits from one person who volunteers to facilitate. This doesn't require expertise — just someone willing to:

- Read the first question aloud
- Make sure quieter voices get space
- Keep the group from getting stuck on one topic
- Wrap up when time is called

As the host, you don't need to facilitate every group — just brief your table volunteers before the breakout starts.

---

## Opening a breakout

> "We have about 45 minutes. We're going to work through a couple of questions together — there are no right answers. The goal is just to think out loud with each other. Who wants to read the first question?"

---

## Keeping it moving

- If the group is stuck: "What would you say to someone who had never thought about this before?"
- If one person is dominating: "Let's hear from someone we haven't heard from yet."
- If the conversation is too abstract: "Can you give a specific example from your own life or work?"
- If you're running short on time: "Last thought on this — then we'll move to the next question."

---

## Closing the breakout

Before reconvening, ask each group to identify:
- One thing someone said that surprised them
- One question the group still has

These surface well when the full room reconvenes.

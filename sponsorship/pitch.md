# Local Sponsor Pitch

HQ does not provide financial support for local events. Chapter leads are responsible for covering venue and food/drink costs through local sponsorship.

The good news: this is a lightweight ask. Most events cost $200–$1,500 depending on size and city.

---

## The ask

Find one local company — ideally a tech company, law firm, VC, or coworking space — willing to:

- **Host** (provide their space for free), OR
- **Sponsor** (cover food + drinks at a third-party venue), OR
- **Both**

In exchange, they get:
- Recognition as the local sponsor at the event (verbal + name tag)
- Logo in local event promotion
- Presence at the event (they can send team members, set up a table if appropriate)
- Association with a global week of events across 100+ cities

---

## Verbal pitch script

Use this when talking to a potential sponsor:

> "We're hosting a Humans in AI Week event here in [city] on [date] — it's part of a global week of simultaneous AI conversations happening in 100+ cities. We're expecting [X] people — [describe your audience: founders, researchers, operators, etc.].
>
> We're looking for a local partner to cover [venue / food and drinks]. The budget is roughly [amount]. In exchange, we'd recognize you as the local sponsor at the event and in all our local promotion.
>
> This is a great way to get in front of [audience] and be part of something that's genuinely historic — the largest coordinated civilian conversation about AI ever organized. Are you interested?"

---

## Who to ask

Best targets:
- **Coworking spaces** — often will host for free in exchange for exposure to your community
- **Tech companies with offices** — happy to host after-hours events
- **VC firms** — highly relevant audience, often have event spaces
- **Law firms (tech-focused)** — sponsor community events regularly
- **AI/tech startups** — want to be associated with the AI conversation

---

## Budget guidance

| Event size | Typical cost |
|------------|-------------|
| 15–30 people | $0–$300 |
| 30–75 people | $300–$700 |
| 75–150 people | $700–$1,500 |
| 150+ people | $1,500+ |

Costs drop dramatically if a sponsor provides their space. Prioritize finding a venue host first, then a separate food/drink sponsor if needed.

# Run of Show Template

> **Note**: For 2026, each of the 6 HAIW formats has its own detailed agenda in [`/formats/`](../formats/). Use those instead of these generic templates. This file is kept as a reference for ground rules and legacy format examples.

Adapt this to your format. Times are approximate — adjust for your event length.

---

## Format A: Panel + Q&A (2.5 hours)

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 6:00 PM | **Doors open** | 30 min | Registration, networking, refreshments |
| 6:30 PM | **Welcome & context** | 8 min | Chapter lead sets the stage. What is HAIW, why we're here, ground rules |
| 6:38 PM | **Panel introductions** | 5 min | Each panelist: name, role, one sentence on why this matters to them |
| 6:43 PM | **Moderated discussion** | 45 min | Use questions from your [discussion guide](../content/discussion-guides/). 3–4 questions, let conversation flow |
| 7:28 PM | **Audience Q&A** | 20 min | Open floor. Moderator filters questions. Keep responses under 2 min |
| 7:48 PM | **Closing** | 5 min | Thank sponsors, panelists, attendees. Share how to stay involved. Photo moment |
| 7:53 PM | **Networking** | 30 min | Open mingling. This is where connections happen |
| 8:23 PM | **Wrap** | — | Venue closes |

## Format B: Roundtable Discussion (2 hours)

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 6:00 PM | **Doors open** | 20 min | Registration, refreshments |
| 6:20 PM | **Welcome & framing** | 10 min | Chapter lead sets context. Introduce the discussion topic and ground rules |
| 6:30 PM | **Round 1: Opening question** | 20 min | Big, open question. Everyone gets a chance to react (popcorn or go-around) |

| 7:45 PM | **Networking** | 15 min | Open conversation |

## Ground rules (share at the start)

Read these aloud or display on screen:

1. **This is a conversation, not a debate.** We're here to understand, not to win.
2. **All perspectives welcome.** You don't need to be technical to participate.
3. **Listen more than you speak.** Make space for voices that aren't usually in this room.
4. **Stay curious.** If someone says something you disagree with, ask a question before reacting.
5. **Chatham House Rule.** You can share what was said, but not who said it.
6. **Phones down.** Be present. You can tweet later.

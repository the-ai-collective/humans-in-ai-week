# AI Prompt Library

Copy-paste these prompts into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant to generate customized HAIW materials for your city. Each prompt is designed to produce ready-to-use output — just fill in your details before pasting.

## How to use

1. Find the prompt you need below
2. Replace the `ALL_CAPS` placeholders with your event details
3. Paste into your AI tool of choice
4. Review and edit the output — AI gets you 80%, you add the last 20%

## 1. Localize your sponsor pitch

Generates a customized sponsor one-pager for a specific local business.

```
I'm a chapter lead for The AI Collective in CITY, COUNTRY. I'm hosting a Humans in AI Week event — a global initiative happening in 200+ cities during June 1–7, 2026.

I need to write a sponsor pitch email for COMPANY_NAME, a COMPANY_DESCRIPTION (e.g., "local coworking space" or "regional tech company").

My event details:
- Date: EVENT_DATE
- Venue: VENUE_NAME (or "still looking for a venue" if you want them to host)
- Expected attendees: EXPECTED_ATTENDANCE
- Format: FORMAT (Demo Night / Research Roundtable / Discussion Meetup / Dinner / Workshop / Hackathon — see formats/)
- Discussion topic: TRACK_NAME

Sponsorship ask: WHAT_YOU_NEED (e.g., "$500 to cover catering" or "venue space for 100 people" or "$1,000 for venue + food")

Write a short, professional sponsor pitch email. Keep it under 200 words. Lead with what's in it for them — visibility, audience access, community goodwill. Don't be salesy. Include a clear ask and a deadline.
```

## 5. Customize discussion questions

Generates localized discussion prompts based on your city's context.

```
I'm facilitating a Humans in AI Week discussion in CITY, COUNTRY. The track is: TRACK_NAME.

About my city:
- Key industries: INDUSTRIES (e.g., "finance, healthcare, manufacturing")
- AI presence: AI_CONTEXT (e.g., "3 AI startups, a university AI lab, major tech company office")
- Current local AI story: LOCAL_NEWS (e.g., "a local hospital just adopted AI diagnostics" or "factory automation is a hot topic")
- Audience: AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTION (e.g., "mostly tech professionals" or "mixed — students, professionals, retirees" or "heavily academic")

Generate 5 discussion questions that:
1. Connect the track theme to my city's specific context
2. Start broad and get more specific/local
3. Include a moderator tip for each (how to handle the discussion)
4. End with an action-oriented question ("what should we do?")
5. Are accessible to non-technical attendees

Also suggest one audience participation activity that fits this track and audience.
```

```
- Panelists: PANELIST_NAMES_AND_TITLES (or "to be announced")
- Cost: Free
- Sponsor: SPONSOR_NAME (or "TBD")

Write the event description in this structure:
1. One-line hook (what this is and why it matters)
2. Short paragraph: what Humans in AI Week is (200+ cities, global initiative)
3. What to expect at this specific event (format, topic, who's speaking)
4. Who should attend (make it inclusive — not just for tech people)
5. Logistics (time, place, free, food/drinks if applicable)
6. Simple CTA

Keep it under 250 words. Tone: inviting, direct, not corporate.
```

Generates a theme framing, challenge prompts, and judging rubric for Hackathon events.

```
I'm hosting a Humans in AI Week Hackathon in CITY on EVENT_DATE. It's an 8-hour build day — teams form in the morning, hack all day, present at the end.

   - Solvable in 6 hours by a small team
   - Connected to the theme
   - Accessible to mixed-skill teams (not just senior engineers)
3. Judging criteria with a scoring rubric (5 dimensions, 1–5 scale each):
   - Impact, Creativity, Technical execution, Presentation, Theme alignment

4. A suggested team formation process (how to help strangers form teams at the start)
```

Generates a session plan, tool selection rationale, and pre-event attendee email for Workshop events.

```
I'm hosting a Humans in AI Week Workshop in CITY on EVENT_DATE. It's a 3-hour hands-on session where attendees build something with AI tools.

My event details:

- Discussion topic (optional): TRACK_NAME
- Tools available: TOOLS (e.g., "ChatGPT and Claude accounts" or "Python environment with API access" or "no-code tools only")

Generate:

1. A workshop curriculum outline with two sessions:
   - Session 1 (45 min): Guided build — everyone follows along to create SOMETHING_SPECIFIC
   - Session 2 (45 min): Open build — participants extend or create their own project
   - Include timing for each segment within the sessions
2. Tool selection rationale: why the chosen tool(s) work for this audience and skill level
3. A pre-event email to attendees (under 150 words) that tells them:
   - What to install or set up before arriving
   - What skill level is expected (reassure beginners)
   - What they'll walk away with
4. Three backup activities if the main tool has connectivity issues
```

## Tips for better AI output

1. **Be specific.** "A tech company" gives worse results than "a 50-person SaaS startup that sells to healthcare companies."
2. **Include real details.** Names, numbers, and local context make the output immediately usable.
3. **Edit the output.** AI gets you to 80% — your local knowledge and voice is the last 20%.
4. **Iterate.** If the first output isn't right, tell the AI what to change: "Make it shorter" / "Less formal" / "Emphasize the free aspect more."
5. **Use your own AI tool.** These prompts work with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM.

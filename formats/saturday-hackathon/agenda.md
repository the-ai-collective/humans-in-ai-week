# Saturday: Hackathon — Agenda

**Duration**: 8 hours | **Ideal size**: 30–100 attendees

The longest and most ambitious format. Teams form, build, and demo — all in one day.

---

## Run of show

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 9:00 AM | **Check-in + breakfast** | 30 min | Registration, coffee, pastries, team formation boards |
| 9:30 AM | **HQ Opening** | 5 min | Read from [`hq-opening.md`](./hq-opening.md) — non-negotiable. Use "today" not "tonight" |
| 9:35 AM | **Theme announcement + team formation** | 25 min | Announce theme/challenges, 60-sec idea pitches, form teams of 3–5 |
| 10:00 AM | **Hack Session 1** | 2.25 hr | Teams build. Mentors circulate. Check-in at 11:00 AM |
| 12:15 PM | **Lunch + lightning talks** | 45 min | Catered lunch. 2–3 five-minute talks from mentors/sponsors on relevant topics |
| 1:00 PM | **Hack Session 2** | 2.25 hr | Teams continue building. Final mentor check-in at 2:15 PM. Submissions close at 3:15 PM |
| 3:15 PM | **Presentations** | 45 min | Each team: 3 min demo + 2 min Q&A. Strict timer. All teams present |
| 4:00 PM | **Judging + prizes** | 20 min | Judges deliberate (or audience votes). Announce winners. Prizes |
| 4:20 PM | **Closing** | 10 min | Thank sponsors, mentors, participants. Share follow-up resources. Group photo |
| 4:30 PM | **Networking + teardown** | 30 min | Open mingling. Begin cleanup |
| 5:00 PM | **End** | — | |

---

## Pre-event setup checklist

- [ ] Test Wi-Fi under load (30+ simultaneous users)
- [ ] Set up team formation board (whiteboard with post-its for idea pitches)
- [ ] Arrange tables for teams (4–5 chairs each, power strips at every table)
- [ ] Set up power strips and extension cords everywhere
- [ ] Prepare judging criteria handout for judges
- [ ] Coordinate breakfast and lunch catering
- [ ] Set up mentor sign-up sheet (time slots)
- [ ] Prepare submission form (Google Form: team name, members, project description, demo link/screenshot)
- [ ] Test projector and screen for presentations
- [ ] Print schedule poster for the wall

---

## Equipment requirements

- Strong Wi-Fi (test under load)
- Power strips at every table
- Projector + screen
- Microphone (for opening, lightning talks, presentations)
- Whiteboard / post-its for team formation and ideation
- Timer for presentations (3 min + 2 min Q&A, visible)
- Printed judging rubric for each judge
- Prizes

---

## Required roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| MC / host | Runs opening, team formation, presentations, closing. Energy setter |
| Mentors (3–5) | Circulate and advise teams. Rotate in shifts to avoid burnout |
| Judges (2–3) | Can overlap with mentors. Evaluate final presentations |
| Timekeeper | Enforces presentation time limits. Manages overall schedule |
| A/V support | Manages projector, audio, Wi-Fi troubleshooting |
| Catering coordinator | Manages breakfast, lunch, snacks timing |
| Photographer/videographer | Captures team building, presentations, winners, group photo |

---

## Topic track overlay

The hackathon theme should align with your chosen track. Frame the theme as a **challenge**, not a topic.

- **Track 4** (Building with AI): "Build an AI tool that solves a problem in [CITY]"
- **Track 3** (AI Safety & Governance): "Build a tool that makes AI more transparent or accountable"
- **Track 6** (AI in Your City): "Build something [CITY] needs"
- **Track 5** (AI Native): "Build a workflow that makes [INDUSTRY] 10x more productive with AI"

---

## Judging criteria (suggested)

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Impact | 25% | Does it solve a real problem? |
| Innovation | 25% | Is the approach creative? |
| Execution | 25% | Does the demo work? |
| Presentation | 25% | Can the team explain it clearly? |

Adjust weights to match your theme. Share criteria with teams at the start so they optimize for the right things.

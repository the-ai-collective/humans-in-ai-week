# Saturday: Hackathon — Best Practices

---

## What makes a great Hackathon

Clear theme, diverse teams, working demos at the end. The theme is the creative constraint — too broad ("build anything with AI") produces unfocused projects; too narrow kills creativity. Sweet spot: **"Build [category] for [audience] using AI."**

---

## Team formation strategy

Don't let people form teams before the event. Use the **pitch-and-form method**:

1. Anyone with an idea gets 60 seconds to pitch it to the room
2. After all pitches, people vote with their feet — walk to the idea that excites you
3. Target teams of **3–5 people**
4. Explicitly encourage mixing technical and non-technical participants — every team needs someone who can tell the story during the demo

If someone doesn't have an idea and doesn't gravitate to a team, have the MC help match them. Nobody sits alone.

---

## Mentor rotation

Assign mentors to **specific time slots**, not all day. Burnout is real.

- 3 mentors × 2-hour shifts covers the day well
- Mentors should ask questions, not give answers: "What problem are you solving?" is better than "Here's how to build it."
- Schedule a formal check-in at 11:00 AM and 2:30 PM so mentors can identify stuck teams

---

## Common mistakes

- **No clear theme** — Projects end up unfocused. The theme is the creative constraint.
- **Teams too large** — 5+ people means some sit idle. Cap at 5.
- **No non-technical roles** — Design, storytelling, and pitching matter as much as code. Say this explicitly.
- **Wi-Fi failure** — Test under load. Have hotspots ready.
- **Not enough power outlets** — 30 people with laptops need a lot of power. Bring extension cords.
- **Running behind schedule** — Strict time enforcement is essential, especially for presentations. Start the timer before the team is ready if you have to.
- **Prizes that are too valuable** — Shifts focus from learning to winning. Keep prizes fun.

---

## Prize suggestions

Keep it fun, not high-stakes:
- **Best Demo** — trophy or plaque
- **Audience Favorite** — voted by attendees
- **Most Creative Use of AI** — judges' pick
- **"Ship It" Award** — most production-ready project
- Prizes: bragging rights, small gift cards ($25–50), mentor office hours, sponsor swag

Do NOT offer large cash prizes. It changes the dynamic from collaborative to competitive.

---

## Scaling guidance

| Size | Adjustments |
|------|------------|
| Under 30 | 5–6 teams, casual vibe, skip formal judging — peer feedback instead |
| 30–60 | Standard format as described above |
| 60–100 | Add multiple tracks/themes, more mentors, consider parallel presentation rooms |
| 100+ | Needs a dedicated operations team, multiple judging panels, larger venue |

---

## All-day venue needs

Full-day access is non-negotiable. Your venue must have:
- **8+ hours** of uninterrupted access (9 AM – 5:30 PM minimum)
- Kitchen or catering staging area for breakfast and lunch
- Breakout rooms for teams wanting quiet space
- Reliable Wi-Fi (test under load before the event)
- Enough power outlets for everyone (laptops + chargers)
- Climate control — rooms get hot with lots of people and laptops
- Consider outdoor space or lounge area for breaks

---

## Budget note

Second highest cost after Dinner. Budget for: venue (full day), breakfast, lunch, snacks/drinks, prizes. Typical range: **$800–$3,000**. Position with sponsors as "presenting sponsor of the hackathon" — high visibility, all day.

# Wednesday: Discussion Meetup — Agenda

**Duration**: 2 hours | **Ideal size**: 15–60 attendees

The most intimate format. No stage, no panel — everyone is a participant.

---

## Run of show

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 6:00 PM | **Doors open** | 20 min | Registration, refreshments. Arrange chairs in a circle or semicircle |
| 6:20 PM | **HQ Opening** | 5 min | Read from [`hq-opening.md`](./hq-opening.md) — non-negotiable |
| 6:25 PM | **Framing** | 5 min | Facilitator introduces the topic track, sets ground rules, explains the format |
| 6:30 PM | **Round 1: Opening question** | 20 min | Big, open question from discussion guide. Go-around or popcorn style. Everyone speaks |
| 6:50 PM | **Round 2: Deep dive** | 25 min | More specific question. Facilitator draws connections between responses |
| 7:15 PM | **Round 3: Local lens** | 20 min | "What does this mean for [CITY]?" Bring the conversation home |
| 7:35 PM | **Synthesis** | 10 min | Facilitator summarizes themes. "What did we hear? What surprised us? What do we do next?" |
| 7:45 PM | **Networking** | 15 min | Open conversation. Connections happen here |
| 8:00 PM | **Wrap** | — | Venue closes |

---

## Pre-event setup checklist

- [ ] Arrange seating in a circle — no "front of room"
- [ ] Prepare discussion questions on printed cards
- [ ] Set up name tags (first name only encouraged)
- [ ] Test audio if room holds 30+ people
- [ ] Prepare a flip chart or whiteboard for synthesis notes
- [ ] Set out refreshments before start time

---

## Equipment requirements

Minimal — this is the simplest format to produce.

- Chairs arranged in a circle
- Name tags
- Discussion question printouts (one per participant or displayed on flip chart)
- Flip chart or whiteboard (optional, for synthesis)
- Single microphone only if 30+ people

---

## Required roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Facilitator | THE key role. Guides discussion, draws out quiet voices, manages time. Sits in the circle as an equal |
| Note-taker | Captures themes and key quotes for the post-event report |
| Photographer | Minimum photos: circle setup, engaged participants, flip chart notes |

---

## Topic track overlay

Discussion Meetup works with **any track**. Best pairings:

- **Track 1** (What It Means to Be Human in the AI Era) — most philosophical, ideal for intimate settings
- **Track 6** (AI in Your City & Industry) — local context makes discussion personal
- **Track 2** (AI and the Future of Work) — universally relatable

Pick the track that resonates most with your community and use its discussion questions directly for Rounds 1–3.

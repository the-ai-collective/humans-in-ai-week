# Wednesday: Discussion Meetup — Best Practices

---

## What makes a great Discussion Meetup

The facilitator is an equal participant, not a moderator on stage. Circle seating eliminates hierarchy. Smaller is better — 15–40 people is the sweet spot. Silence is okay. The best discussions breathe.

---

## Facilitator as equal

Sit in the circle. Share your own answer first to model vulnerability. Use "I noticed..." not "The consensus is..." Your job is to ask questions, manage time, and draw out quiet voices — not to be the expert.

Tips:
- Ask the opening question, then answer it yourself first. This sets the tone.
- Use people's names when connecting ideas: "That connects to what Maria said earlier about..."
- If a thread is rich, let it run. If it stalls, move to the next question.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Too many people** — Over 50 loses intimacy. Split into breakout circles if needed.
- **Traditional classroom seating** — Rows kill participation. Circle or nothing.
- **Facilitator talking too much** — Your share of talk time should be under 15%.
- **Not enforcing go-around on Round 1** — Some people won't speak unless explicitly invited. A go-around in Round 1 gives everyone permission to participate for the rest of the night.
- **Skipping synthesis** — The "so what" moment matters. Without it, people leave feeling unresolved.

---

## Circle management

| Situation | What to say |
|-----------|------------|
| Someone dominates | "Great point. Let's hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet." |
| Silence after a question | "Take a moment to think. No rush." |
| Tangent | "Interesting — let me park that and come back. The question on the table is..." |
| Disagreement escalates | "I hear two different perspectives. Can we sit with both for a moment?" |
| Someone hasn't spoken | "We haven't heard from you yet — would you like to share?" (Never force.) |

---

## Scaling guidance

| Size | Format |
|------|--------|
| Under 20 | Single circle, go-around for all rounds |
| 20–40 | Single circle, popcorn style after Round 1 go-around |
| 40–60 | Fishbowl: inner circle discusses, outer circle observes, rotate halfway |
| Over 60 | Split into multiple circles with a facilitator per circle, do report-backs at the end |

# Friday: Workshop — Agenda

**Duration**: 3 hours | **Ideal size**: 20–60 attendees

Everyone builds something. By the end, every person in the room has a working project.

---

## Run of show

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 6:00 PM | **Doors open** | 20 min | Registration, Wi-Fi info posted, verify attendees have required tools installed |
| 6:20 PM | **HQ Opening** | 5 min | Read from [`hq-opening.md`](./hq-opening.md) — non-negotiable |
| 6:25 PM | **Tool overview** | 15 min | Instructor demos the tool/platform. What it does, why it matters, what we'll build tonight |
| 6:40 PM | **Hands-on Session 1** | 45 min | Guided exercise. Step-by-step. Instructor walks through, attendees follow along. Helpers circulate |
| 7:25 PM | **Break** | 10 min | Stretch, refuel, troubleshoot stragglers |
| 7:35 PM | **Hands-on Session 2: Open build** | 45 min | Attendees build their own project using what they learned. Helpers available. Pair beginners with experienced |
| 8:20 PM | **Show & tell** | 15 min | 4–6 volunteers share what they built (2–3 min each). Celebrate all levels |
| 8:35 PM | **Discussion** | 15 min | Reflect: "What surprised you? What would you build next? How does this change your work?" |
| 8:50 PM | **Networking** | 10 min | Exchange contacts, continue conversations |
| 9:00 PM | **Wrap** | — | Venue closes |

---

## Pre-event setup checklist

- [ ] Test Wi-Fi under load — critical. Have a backup mobile hotspot
- [ ] Prepare step-by-step written instructions (attendees WILL fall behind the live demo)
- [ ] Set up power strips at every table
- [ ] Test projector/screen visibility from all seats
- [ ] Verify tool access: API keys, accounts, free tiers all working
- [ ] Send "pre-flight" email with install instructions **48 hours before**
- [ ] Have USB drives with offline installers as backup

---

## Equipment requirements

- Projector + screen (large, readable from back of room)
- Strong Wi-Fi — test with 30+ simultaneous connections
- Power strips and extension cords at every table
- Whiteboard for troubleshooting common issues
- Microphone for instructor
- Printed or digital step-by-step guide for each attendee

---

## Required roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Instructor | Teaches the tool. Must be patient, encouraging, comfortable going slow |
| Helpers / TAs (2–3) | Circulate during hands-on sessions. Troubleshoot individual issues. 1 helper per 10–15 attendees |
| Timekeeper | Keeps sessions on track. Signals instructor at transition points |
| A/V support | Manages projector, screen sharing, recordings |
| Photographer | Captures hands-on work, show & tell, engaged participants |

---

## Topic track overlay

Workshop maps directly to **Track 4 (Building with AI)** or **Track 5 (How to Be AI Native)**.

- **Track 4**: Build something — API integration, AI agent, application
- **Track 5**: Use AI in daily work — prompt engineering, workflow automation, productivity tools

Choose a tool that aligns with your track. The discussion segment (8:40 PM) ties the hands-on experience back to the track's bigger themes.

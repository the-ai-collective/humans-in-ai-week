# Friday: Workshop — Best Practices

---

## What makes a great Workshop

Everyone builds something. Even beginners leave with a working project. The instructor's energy sets the tone — be encouraging, celebrate mistakes as learning moments, go slow on the basics. The best workshops feel like a group adventure, not a classroom lecture.

---

## Pre-event setup is everything

- Send install instructions **48 hours before** via email
- Test Wi-Fi with the actual tool under realistic load
- Have offline fallbacks for every step
- Prepare written step-by-step guides — people WILL fall behind the live demo
- Record the instructor's screen so latecomers can catch up during break

---

## Wi-Fi backup plan

Corporate/venue Wi-Fi often blocks ports or has captive portals. Test with the actual tool beforehand.

**If Wi-Fi fails:**
1. Switch to mobile hotspot (have one ready)
2. Pivot to pre-recorded demo + discussion
3. Switch to offline-capable exercises
4. Worst case: pair people who have mobile data with those who don't

---

## Pair programming encouraged

Explicitly pair experienced attendees with beginners. Say it out loud: "If you've used this tool before, sit next to someone who hasn't." This creates natural mentorship and prevents the instructor from being the bottleneck when 20 people raise their hands at once.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Assuming everyone has the tool installed** — They won't. Budget 15 min for setup help.
- **Going too fast** — Slow down 50% from what feels natural. When in doubt, slower.
- **Not enough helpers** — 1 helper per 10–15 attendees minimum. Recruit from your chapter.
- **Wi-Fi failure with no backup** — Always have Plan B (hotspot) and Plan C (offline exercises).
- **Choosing a tool that requires paid accounts** — Free tier only. No credit cards.
- **No written instructions** — The live demo alone is not enough. People need something to reference.

---

## Tool selection criteria

- Must have a **free tier** (no credit card required)
- Must run on **Mac, Windows, and Linux**
- Must produce a **visible result in under 45 minutes**
- Must not require advanced programming knowledge (unless your chapter is explicitly technical)
- Bonus: tool with good documentation and community support

---

## Scaling guidance

| Size | Adjustments |
|------|------------|
| Under 30 | Single instructor, 1–2 helpers. Intimate, lots of individual attention |
| 30–60 | Add more helpers. Consider two parallel tracks: beginner and advanced |
| 60+ | Multiple rooms or livestream the instructor with helpers in each section |

---

## Suggested topic track pairings

- **Primary**: Track 4 (Building with AI) — build-focused tool: API, framework, AI agent
- **Also strong**: Track 5 (How to Be AI Native) — productivity tool: prompt engineering, workflow automation
- Match the tool to the track. The discussion at the end ties the hands-on experience to bigger themes.

# Monday: Demo Night — Best Practices

---

## What makes a great Demo Night

Live demos beat slides every time. Aim for diversity of projects — not all chatbots. Mix solo hackers with startup teams. The energy comes from variety: a nurse who automated her charting workflow is as compelling as a developer who built an AI agent. Surprise the audience.

---

## Sourcing demos

Open a submission form **4–6 weeks before** your event. Share it in your chapter Slack/Discord, local tech meetups, university CS departments, and on social media. Aim for 10–12 submissions, select 5–8 for the night. **Have 2 backup presenters** in case of no-shows.

### Demo submission form template

Include these fields:
- Name and contact email
- Project name
- One-sentence description
- Tech stack used
- Demo requirements (needs internet? special hardware? specific browser?)
- Estimated demo time (max 5 minutes)

---

## Common mistakes

- **Not enforcing time limits** — Use a visible countdown. When time is up, it's up. Practice this in advance so it's not awkward.
- **Letting Q&A run over** — 2 minutes. Period. Remaining questions go to networking time.
- **Too many demos** — Audience attention drops after 8 demos. Quality over quantity.
- **No backup plan for technical failures** — Have presenters pre-record a 2-minute screen capture. If live demo crashes, play the backup.
- **Forgetting adapters/dongles** — Bring HDMI, USB-C, and DisplayPort adapters. Test with each presenter's laptop before doors open.

---

## Scaling guidance

| Size | Adjustments |
|------|------------|
| Under 50 | Intimate. Skip formal voting — do group discussion instead of panel |
| 50–100 | Standard format. Works perfectly as designed |
| 100–150 | Add a second screen. Use wireless mic. Consider lightning rounds (3 min each) |
| 150+ | Split into two rooms with different demo tracks, or add a pre-selection round |

---

## Suggested topic track pairings

- **Primary**: Track 4 (Building with AI) — the demos are live examples of building
- **Secondary**: Track 5 (How to Be AI Native) — demos show AI integration into real workflows
- The demos ARE the content. The track overlay shapes the post-demo panel conversation.

---

## Backup plan

If a demo fails live, the presenter does a 2-minute walkthrough with screenshots or pre-recorded video. Always ask presenters to prepare this in advance. "Hope for the best, prepare for the worst" is the Demo Night motto.

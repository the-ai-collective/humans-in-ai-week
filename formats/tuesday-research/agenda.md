# Tuesday: Research Roundtable — Agenda

**Duration**: 2.5 hours | **Ideal size**: 30–100 attendees

---

## Run of show

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 6:00 PM | **Doors open** | 30 min | Registration, networking, refreshments |
| 6:30 PM | **HQ Opening** | 5 min | Read from [`hq-opening.md`](./hq-opening.md) — non-negotiable |
| 6:35 PM | **Panel introductions** | 10 min | Each panelist: name, institution, research focus, one finding that keeps them up at night |
| 6:45 PM | **Moderated discussion** | 45 min | 4–5 questions from discussion guide. Let researchers go deep but keep it accessible |
| 7:30 PM | **Audience Q&A** | 20 min | Open floor. Moderator filters for quality. Encourage curious questions over gotcha questions |
| 7:50 PM | **Closing** | 5 min | Key takeaways, recommended readings, how to follow the panelists' work |
| 7:55 PM | **Networking** | 35 min | Encourage attendees to approach panelists directly |
| 8:30 PM | **Wrap** | — | Venue closes |

---

## Pre-event setup checklist

- [ ] Test A/V system (microphones, projector)
- [ ] Set up panel seating — half-circle facing audience, not behind a table
- [ ] Prepare moderator question cards (printed, large font)
- [ ] Set up name placards for each panelist
- [ ] Test any presentation equipment for papers or slides referenced
- [ ] Prepare recommended reading handout (optional but appreciated)

---

## Equipment requirements

- Microphones — 1 per panelist + 1 roaming for audience Q&A
- Projector (for any slides or papers referenced during discussion)
- Name placards for panelists
- Timer (visible to moderator)

---

## Required roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Moderator | Guides discussion, translates jargon, manages time and audience Q&A |
| Timekeeper | Keeps discussion segments on track. Signals moderator at 5-min and 1-min marks |
| A/V tech | Manages microphones, projector, any recordings |
| Photographer | Captures panelists, audience engagement, crowd shots |

---

## Topic track overlay

Research Roundtable pairs naturally with **Track 3 (AI Safety & Governance)** or **Track 1 (What It Means to Be Human in the AI Era)**. Choose your track first, then recruit panelists whose research aligns with those themes. The track's discussion questions become your moderator's roadmap.

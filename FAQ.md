# Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions every chapter lead, team member, and partner asks.

---

## Event basics

### What exactly is Humans in AI Week?

A coordinated global week where every AI Collective chapter hosts a public event about AI — same week, same conversation, 200+ cities. It's not a conference. It's a convening — panels, roundtables, and open discussions about where AI is going and who gets a say.

### When is it?

June 1–7, 2026. Your event can be on any day during that week.

### How long should my event be?

It depends on your format. Discussion Meetup is 2 hours, Demo Night and Research Roundtable are 2.5 hours, Dinner and Workshop are 3 hours, and Hackathon is a full 8-hour day. See [`/formats/`](./formats/) for all six formats with detailed agendas.

### Do I have to use one of the six discussion tracks?

No, but we strongly recommend it. The tracks give you ready-made discussion questions, moderator tips, and audience participation ideas. You can combine multiple tracks or add your own questions. The point is: don't start from a blank page.

### Can I do a virtual-only event?

Yes. If you can't secure a venue or your community is geographically spread out, a virtual event counts. Use the same discussion guides. We'll provide guidance on the global virtual platform once it's confirmed (see [Pending Assets](./README.md#pending-assets)).

### Can I do a hybrid event (in-person + livestream)?

Yes. If you have the A/V capability, livestreaming expands your reach. Just make sure the in-room experience isn't degraded by managing a stream.

## Planning & logistics

### How many people should I plan for?

Target 50–200. Under 50 is fine — a great 30-person roundtable is better than a half-empty 200-person room. Over 200, consider the hybrid format with breakout tables.

### What if I can't find a venue?

Try these in order:
1. Ask a local sponsor to provide space (use the [sponsor pitch](./sponsorship/local-sponsor-pitch.md))
2. Contact a local university, library, or coworking space (use the [community partner kit](./sponsorship/local-community-partner-kit.md))
3. Ask your AIC chapter members — someone's company probably has an event space
4. Go virtual

### What if I can't find a sponsor?

It's okay. Many events can be hosted at $0 cost if you get a free venue and skip catering. The sponsor pitch is a tool, not a requirement. Don't let funding block you from hosting.

### Do I need to charge attendees?

No. HAIW events should be free. If you have costs to cover, find a sponsor. Don't charge a ticket price — it kills accessibility, which is the whole point.

### What's the Luma calendar?

Luma is the event registration platform we use. Once you create your event on Luma, submit the link to catherine@aicollective.com so it appears on the global HAIW calendar.

## Content & format

### What format works best?

HAIW has 6 day-specific formats. Depends on your community:
- **Demo Night** (Monday) — best for builder communities with active projects
- **Research Roundtable** (Tuesday) — best for university towns and research communities
- **Discussion Meetup** (Wednesday) — best for first-time hosts, smaller groups, any chapter
- **Dinner** (Thursday) — best for well-funded chapters and executive audiences
- **Workshop** (Friday) — best for technical communities who want to build hands-on
- **Hackathon** (Saturday) — best for large tech chapters and university partnerships

See the [format decision matrix](./formats/README.md) for a detailed comparison by chapter size, community type, and budget.

### What if my venue is only available on a different day?

No problem. The day assignments are defaults, not requirements. If your venue is only available on Wednesday but you want to run a Demo Night — do it. Use the correct format files regardless of which day you run.

### What is the HQ Opening Script?

Every HAIW event worldwide opens with the same 5-minute script. It connects your local event to the global movement — "right now, in 200+ cities..." Each format has its own version in `formats/{format}/hq-opening.md` with a shared opening, a format-specific bridge, and a localization prompt for you to fill in. This is non-negotiable — it's what makes HAIW feel like one global event.

### Can I use AI to plan my entire event?

Yes. Give your AI agent (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) access to this repo and point it to [`CLAUDE.md`](./CLAUDE.md). It contains a complete instruction set for generating your task list, timeline, and customized materials. The [AI Prompt Library](./ai-prompts/README.md) also has 13 standalone prompts if you prefer to generate content piece by piece.

### How do I find panelists?

Look for people who are *affected by* AI, not just people building it. A nurse, a teacher, a journalist, a city council member — they bring perspectives the room doesn't usually hear. Mix in 1–2 technical voices for balance.

Use the [Speaker Invite template](./marketing/email-templates/speaker-invite.md) or the [AI prompt to generate a personalized invite](./ai-prompts/README.md#3-write-speaker-invite-emails).

### Do panelists need to prepare slides?

No. HAIW events are conversations, not presentations. Share the discussion questions with panelists a week before so they can think about their answers. That's all the prep they need.

### What if I'm not a good moderator?

Read the [Facilitation Tips](./content/facilitation-tips.md). The key insight: your job is to ask questions and manage time, not to be an expert. The best moderators talk the least.

If you're really uncomfortable, recruit a co-host who's comfortable facilitating and focus on logistics.

### Can I add my own discussion questions?

Yes. The guides are starting points, not scripts. If something is locally relevant — a recent AI news story, a local company making changes, a policy debate — weave it in.

## Sponsorship & partnerships

### Do I have to find a sponsor?

No, but we strongly recommend it. The goal is $0 out-of-pocket for chapter leads. Even an in-kind sponsor (free venue or food) makes a big difference.

### How much should I ask for?

Most events cost $200–$1,700 depending on your city. See the [budget framework](./chapter-playbook/event-ops-runbook.md#budget-framework). Ask for what you need — don't inflate.

### What's the difference between a sponsor and a community partner?

- **Sponsor** = gives money or in-kind support (venue, food). Gets branding and speaking time.
- **Community partner** = cross-promotes to their audience. Gets co-branding and reserved seats. No money changes hands.

You want both.

### Can a sponsor give a sales pitch during the event?

No. Presenting sponsors get a 3-minute opening remark — that's it. This is a community conversation, not a vendor event. Be clear about this upfront and sponsors will respect it.

## Post-event

### What do I need to submit after the event?

A [Post-Event Report](./chapter-playbook/post-event-report-template.md) within 48 hours. It covers attendance, themes discussed, notable quotes, photos, and feedback. This data is how we tell the global story — "200 cities said X."

### How many photos should I take?

Minimum 10: crowd shots, panelists speaking, candid conversations, venue/signage, sponsor branding. Assign someone to this — don't try to moderate and photograph at the same time.

### What happens after HAIW?

The global team will compile reports from all 200+ cities into a public narrative — the themes, the quotes, the data. This becomes press material, partner reports, and the foundation for future initiatives. Your report contributes directly to this.
